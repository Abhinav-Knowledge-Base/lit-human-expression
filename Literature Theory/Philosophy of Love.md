# Philosophy of Love: A Comparative Analysis

What is love? This document compares definitions from spiritual, psychological, and literary perspectives.

## 1. Jalal ad-Din Muhammad Rumi: Divine Union
- **Nature:** Spiritual and transcendental.
- **View:** Love dissolves the ego; the lover and Beloved become one. It is a path to God.

## 2. Sigmund Freud: Psychological Drive
- **Nature:** Instinctual and biological.
- **View:** Love is the expression of libido (sexual energy) and is often shaped by unconscious childhood desires.

## 3. William Shakespeare: Human Contradiction
- **Nature:** Emotional and unstable.
- **View:** Love is beautiful but flawed. It can be intense passion (*Romeo and Juliet*), jealousy (*Othello*), or irrational chaos (*A Midsummer Night's Dream*).

## 4. Plato: Philosophical Ascent
- **Nature:** Intellectual evolution.
- **View:** Love begins with physical attraction but rises to love of the mind and finally to pure truth and beauty.

## 5. Friedrich Nietzsche: Power Dynamic
- **Nature:** Will to power.
- **View:** Love can be a form of self-assertion or a desire for possession and dominance, often hiding a power struggle.

## 6. Erich Fromm: Learned Skill
- **Nature:** Mature practice.
- **View:** Love is not a feeling but a skill requiring discipline, responsibility, and knowledge. It is an act of growth.
