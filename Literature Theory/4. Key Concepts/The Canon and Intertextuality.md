# The Canon and Intertextuality: The Relationships Between Texts

These concepts explore how literature is valued and how texts communicate with each other.

## 1. The Literary Canon
- **Focus:** The "best" and most influential works of literature, as determined by tradition and institutions (schools, critics, etc.).
- **Debate:** Who decides what belongs in the canon? Critiques of the "Western Canon" for excluding diverse voices (e.g., Post-Colonial and Feminist critiques).
- **The "Great Divide":** The historical separation between "High Art" and "Pop Culture."

## 2. Intertextuality (Julia Kristeva)
- **Focus:** The idea that no text is an island; every text is a mosaic of citations and influences.
- **Forms of Intertextuality:**
    - **Allusion:** Direct or indirect reference to another work.
    - **Parody:** A humorous imitation of a style or work.
    - **Pastiche:** A combination of styles or genres within a single work.
- **Significance:** Meaning is not just in the text, but in the space *between* texts.
