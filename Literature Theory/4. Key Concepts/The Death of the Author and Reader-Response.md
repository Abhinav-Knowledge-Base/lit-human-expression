# The Death of the Author and Reader-Response Theory

These concepts explore where meaning resides: in the author, the text, or the reader?

## 1. The Death of the Author (Roland Barthes)
- **Focus:** A radical rejection of authorial intent. The author is merely the "scriptor" of the text, not its ultimate source of meaning.
- **"The birth of the reader must be at the cost of the death of the Author":** Once a text is published, it enters a "web of signs" where the reader’s interpretation is what creates meaning.

## 2. Reader-Response Theory (Wolfgang Iser, Stanley Fish)
- **Focus:** Analyzing how different readers interpret the same text in different ways.
- **The Gap:** The idea that a text contains "gaps" or "indeterminacies" that the reader must fill with their own imagination and background.
- **Interpretive Communities:** Our interpretations are shaped by the social groups we belong to (e.g., a teacher vs. a student).
