# Hegemony and Power: The Politics of Representation

These concepts explore how literature reflects and shapes social and political power.

## 1. Hegemony (Antonio Gramsci)
- **Focus:** How the ruling class maintains dominance by persuading the rest of society that its values are "natural" or "common sense."
- **Literary Application:** Analyzing how certain stories and characters prioritize white, male, or upper-class perspectives as the "default."

## 2. The Relationship Between Language and Power
- **Discourse (Michel Foucault):** How language and the institutions that control it (schools, state, etc.) define what is "true" and what is "meaningful."
- **Power as Productive:** Power doesn't just "oppress"; it produces the very identities and categories we use to understand ourselves.
