# Romanticism (Late 18th to Mid-19th Century)

**Romanticism** was an intellectual and artistic movement that originated in Europe toward the end of the 18th century as a reaction against the rationalism of the Enlightenment and the Industrial Revolution.

## 核心 Tenets (Key Features)
1. **Emphasis on Emotion:** Validation of intense emotion as an authentic source of aesthetic experience (awe, horror, terror).
2. **Nature as Sublime:** Nature is seen as a powerful, untamed force rather than a machine.
3. **The Individual & The Imagination:** Celebration of the "solitary genius" and the creative power of the mind.
4. **Interest in the Past:** Fascination with the medieval, the gothic, and folklore.
5. **Rebellion against Social Convention:** Emphasis on personal freedom and spontaneity.

## Key Thinkers & Writers
- **Poetry:** William Wordsworth, S.T. Coleridge (English), Victor Hugo (French), Goethe (German).
- **Philosophy:** Jean-Jacques Rousseau (Focus on "noble savage" and nature).

## Legacy
Romanticism paved the way for the celebration of subjectivity and the "I" in literature, influencing virtually all subsequent poetry and fiction.
