# Postmodernism (Mid-20th Century to Present)

**Postmodernism** is a highly skeptical and playful movement that questions the possibility of ultimate truth or "grand narratives."

## Core Tenets
1. **Irony and Playfulness:** Using humor and parody to deconstruct serious subjects.
2. **Metafiction:** Fiction that talks about itself being fiction (breaking the fourth wall).
3. **Intertextuality:** The relationship between texts; the idea that every text is a mosaic of citations.
4. **Rejection of Grand Narratives:** Disbelief in universal truths (like science, progress, or religion).
5. **Pastiche:** Combining different styles and genres (high culture vs. pop culture).

## Key Thinkers
- **Jean-François Lyotard:** Defined postmodernism as "incredulity toward meta-narratives."
- **Roland Barthes:** "The Death of the Author."
- **Jorge Luis Borges:** Anticipated postmodernism with his philosophical stories.
