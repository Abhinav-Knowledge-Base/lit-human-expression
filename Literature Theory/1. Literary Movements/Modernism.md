# Modernism (Late 19th up to Mid-20th Century)

**Modernism** was a radical break from traditional forms of expression, prompted by the disillusionment following World War I and the rise of urbanization and technology.

## Core Tenets
1. **"Make It New":** Ezra Pound’s famous slogan emphasizing the need to break with 19th-century traditions.
2. **Fragmentation:** Use of non-linear narratives and "stream of consciousness" (displaying the chaos of human thought).
3. **Subjectivity:** Rejection of objective reality in favor of personal, internal experience.
4. **Disillusionment:** Themes of alienation, isolation, and the loss of traditional values.
5. **Form over Content:** The way something is written is as important as what is being said.

## Key Writers
- **James Joyce:** *Ulysses* (Stream of consciousness).
- **Virginia Woolf:** *Mrs. Dalloway*.
- **T.S. Eliot:** *The Waste Land*.
- **Franz Kafka:** Exploring the absurdity of modern bureaucracy.
