# Realism and Naturalism (Mid to Late 19th Century)

**Realism** emerged as a counter-movement to Romanticism, focusing on the everyday lives of ordinary people and the social conditions of the time.

## 1. Realism
- **Focus:** Representing life exactly as it is, without idealizing or romanticizing it.
- **Character over Plot:** Emphasis on complex, middle-class characters and their social interactions.
- **Objectivity:** The narrator often takes a "fly-on-the-wall" approach.
- **Key Writers:** Gustave Flaubert, Leo Tolstoy, Fyodor Dostoevsky.

## 2. Naturalism
- **Focus:** An extreme form of realism influenced by Darwin’s theory of evolution.
- **Determinism:** Characters are shaped by their heredity and environment. Life is seen as a struggle for survival.
- **Scientific Method:** Writers like Émile Zola described their work as "experimental novels."
- **Key Writers:** Émile Zola, Stephen Crane.
