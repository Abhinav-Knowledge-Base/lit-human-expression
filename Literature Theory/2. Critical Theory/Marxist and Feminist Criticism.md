# Marxist and Feminist Criticism: Political Theories of Literature

These theories focus on power dynamics and social structures within literature.

## 1. Marxist Criticism
- **Focus:** Literature as a product of class struggle and economic conditions.
- **Base vs. Superstructure:** The economy (base) shapes culture and literature (superstructure).
- **Hegemony (Gramsci):** How the ruling class maintains power through cultural values and storytelling.
- **Ideology:** Analyzing how texts reinforce or challenge the status quo.

## 2. Feminist Criticism
- **Focus:** The representation of women and the critique of patriarchal power.
- **The Male Gaze:** Analyzing how women are often portrayed as objects for male pleasure.
- **Gynocriticism:** The study of women's writing and the reclamation of female voices in the literary canon.
- **Essentialism vs. Social Construction:** Debating whether "femininity" is biological or a social performance.
