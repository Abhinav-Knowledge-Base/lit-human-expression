# Structuralism and Post-Structuralism

These theories focus on the systems of language and signs that allow meaning to be created.

## 1. Structuralism
- **Focus:** The underlying structures of language (the "code") that govern human culture. Meaning is generated by difference (e.g., "hot" only means something in relation to "cold").
- **Key Thinker:** Ferdinand de Saussure (The Sign = Signifier + Signified).
- **Literary Application:** Analyzing the "grammar" of stories (Narratology).

## 2. Post-Structuralism (Deconstruction)
- **Focus:** Questioning the stability of these structures. Language is seen as fluid and "slippery."
- **Key Thinker:** Jacques Derrida.
- **Deconstruction:** The process of showing how a text always undermines its own logic and collapses into multiple contradictions (Aporia).
- **The Death of the Author (Roland Barthes):** The idea that the author has no authority over the text once it is published; the text belongs to the reader and the language itself.
