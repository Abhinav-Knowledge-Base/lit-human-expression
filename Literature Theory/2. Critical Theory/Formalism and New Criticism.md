# Formalism and New Criticism

**Formalism** and **New Criticism** are schools of literary theory that focus on the text itself, independent of its historical context, author’s biography, or social background.

## 核心 Tenets
1. **Close Reading:** A technique of careful and sustained analysis of a text's specific wording, structure, and imagery.
2. **The Intention Paradox:** The "intentional fallacy" suggests that the author's original intent is irrelevant to the meaning of the work.
3. **The Affective Fallacy:** The idea that a reader's emotional response is not a reliable indicator of a work's meaning.
4. **Unity and Organicism:** A belief that a good poem or novel is a self-contained entity where all parts contribute to a unified whole.

## Key Terminology
- **Defamiliarization (Viktor Shklovsky):** The art of making the familiar look strange, forcing the reader to see the world anew.
- **Ambiguity (William Empson):** Multiple layers of meaning within a single word or phrase.
- **Irony and Paradox (Cleanth Brooks):** The central structural devices of great poetry.
