# Narratology: The Study of Narrative Structure

**Narratology** is the branch of literary theory that analyzes the structure and components of a story.

## 1. Story vs. Plot (Fabula vs. Sjuzhet)
- **Story (Fabula):** The chronological sequence of events as they happen in the characters’ lives.
- **Plot (Sjuzhet):** The artistic arrangement of those events in the narrative (including flashbacks, flash-forwards, etc.).

## 2. Perspective and Focalization (Gérard Genette)
- **Narration:** The act of telling the story (Who speaks?).
- **Focalization:** The perspective through which the story is seen (Who sees?).
    - **Zero Focalization:** Omniscient narrator who knows everything.
    - **Internal Focalization:** The narrator only knows what one specific character knows.
    - **External Focalization:** The narrator describes events only from the outside, like a camera.

## 3. Characters and Functions (Vladimir Propp)
- **Stock Characters:** The Hero, the Villain, the Helper, the Donor.
- **Functions:** Recurring plot elements like "The Departure," "The Test," and "The Return."
