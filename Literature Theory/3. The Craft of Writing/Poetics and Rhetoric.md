# Poetics and Rhetoric: The Architecture of Language

This section focuses on the technical aspects of poetry and persuasive writing.

## 1. Prosody and Metre (Poetics)
- **The Foot:** The basic unit of rhythm (e.g., Iambic, Trochaic).
- **Blank Verse:** Unrhymed iambic pentameter (Shakespeare's primary form).
- **Free Verse:** Poetry that does not follow a specific structure or rhyme scheme.
- **Sonnet Structure:** 14 lines with specific rhyme schemes (Petrarchan vs. Shakespearean).

## 2. Figurative Language (Rhetoric)
- **Metaphor vs. Simile:** Direct comparison vs. comparison using "like" or "as."
- **Metonymy:** Using a related object to represent a whole (e.g., "The White House" for the US government).
- **Alliteration and Assonance:** Repetition of consonant or vowel sounds for musical effect.
- **Irony:** The gap between what is said and what is meant (Verbal, Situational, Dramatic).

## 3. The Power of Persuasion
- **Ethos, Pathos, and Logos:** Aristotle’s three pillars of persuasion (Authority, Emotion, and Logic).
