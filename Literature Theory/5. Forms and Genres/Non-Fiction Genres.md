# Non-Fiction Genres: The Literature of Fact and Thought

**Non-fiction** is writing that is based on facts, real events, and real people.

## 1. Narrative Non-Fiction
- **Biography/Autobiography:** The life story of a person (e.g., [Mahatma Gandhi](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Books/Western/8.%2020th%20Century/Gandhi%20-%20A%20Sublime%20Failure.md)).
- **Memoir:** A selective account of one's memory of their life, often focusing on a specific theme or period.
- **Travel Literature:** Writing about travels and experiences abroad.

## 2. Theoretical & Intellectual Prose
- **Philosophy:** Intellectual inquiry into the nature of knowledge, reality, and existence (e.g., [Immanuel Kant](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Western/5.%20Enlightenment/Immanuel%20Kant.md)).
- **Political Theory:** Analyzing power, government, and society (e.g., [Chanakya's Arthashastra](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Books/Indian/1.%20Ancient%20&%20Classical/Arthashastra.md)).
- **Theological Writing:** Religious texts and studies (e.g., [Maimonides](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Jewish/Maimonides.md)).

## 3. Academic & Informative
- **Journalism/Reportage:** Objective (ideally) reporting on current events.
- **Science & Scientific Prose:** Documentation of empirical study and discovery.
- **Social Sciences:** Economics, Psychology, and Sociology studies (e.g., [Sigmund Freud](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Literature%20Theory/Philosophy%20of%20Love.md)).
