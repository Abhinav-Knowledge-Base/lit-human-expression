# Prose: The Literature of Ordinary Language

**Prose** is the most common form of written and spoken language, lacking the metrical structure of poetry.

## 1. The Novel (The Dominant Modern Form)
- **Definition:** A long, fictional narrative prose.
- **Development:** Evolved from earlier epics and romances, reaching its peak in the 19th Century with writers like [Victor Hugo](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Western/6.%2019th%20Century%20Europe/Victor%20Hugo.md) and [Leo Tolstoy](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Russian/2.%20Golden%20Age/Leo%20Tolstoy.md).
- **Sub-types:** Epistolary (letters), Picaresque, Bildungsroman (coming-of-age).

## 2. Short Story vs. Novella
- **Novella:** Shorter than a novel, longer than a short story (e.g., *The Metamorphosis* by Kafka).
- **Short Story:** A brief work of fiction that can usually be read in one sitting, focusing on a single incident or character (e.g., works by [Jorge Luis Borges](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Latin%20American/Jorge%20Luis%20Borges.md)).

## 3. Non-Fiction Prose
- **The Essay:** A short piece of writing on a particular subject, exploring themes through the author's unique voice (e.g., [Emerson](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Western/7.%2019th%20Century%20America/Ralph%20Waldo%20Emerson.md)).
- **Biography and Memoir:** Narratives based on real life, exploring personal or historical truth.
- **Philosophical and Political Prose:** Intellectual treatises (e.g., [Plato](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Western/1.%20Ancient%20Greece/Plato.md), [Chanakya](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Indian/1.%20Ancient%20&%20Classical/Chanakya.md)).
