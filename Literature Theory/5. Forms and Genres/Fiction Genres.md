# Fiction Genres: The Categories of Narrative Content

**Genre** is a category of literature characterized by similarities in form, style, or subject matter.

## 1. Literary Fiction vs. Genre Fiction
- **Literary Fiction:** Focuses on character depth, style, and complex themes, often pushing the boundaries of form.
- **Genre (Pop) Fiction:** Often prioritizes plot and traditional storytelling structures (Scifi, Fantasy, Mystery).

## 2. Major Genres
- **Realism:** Depicts life as it is (e.g., [Dostoevsky](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Russian/2.%20Golden%20Age/Fyodor%20Dostoevsky.md)).
- **Speculative Fiction:** An umbrella category including Science Fiction, Fantasy, and Dystopia (e.g., [George Orwell](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Western/8.%2020th%20Century/George%20Orwell.md)).
- **Magical Realism:** Realistic narrative with magical elements (e.g., [Gabriel García Márquez](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Latin%20American/Gabriel%20Garc%C3%ADa%20M%C3%A1rquez.md)).
- **Historical Fiction:** Stories set in the past, often blending real and fictional characters.
- **Satire:** Using humor, irony, and exaggeration to criticize society (e.g., [Nikolai Gogol](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Writers/Russian/1.%20Foundation/Nikolai%20Gogol.md)).

## 3. Advanced Genre Concepts
- **Genre Blending:** Hybrid works (e.g., literary science fiction).
- **Sub-Genres:** Cyberpunk, High Fantasy, Cozy Mystery, etc.
