# Types of Writing (Form vs Genre)

People often confuse **Form** and **Genre**. This document separates them to help categorize the library more effectively.

## 🔹 A. Literary Forms (Structure)
These are **HOW** something is written.

### 1️⃣ Poetry
Rhythmic, symbolic language.
- **Epic:** long narrative poems (e.g., *Mahabharata*, *Iliad*).
- **Lyric:** expressed personal emotions (e.g., love poems).
- **Narrative:** tells a story.
- **Haiku:** Japanese form (5-7-5 syllables).

### 2️⃣ Drama / Play
Written for stage performance. (e.g., *Hamlet*).

### 3️⃣ Prose
Direct, ordinary language without metrical structure.
- **Novel:** long fictional narrative.
- **Novella:** short novel.
- **Short story**
- **Essay**
- **Biography / Autobiography / Memoir**
- **Letter (Epistolary form)**
- **Travel writing**
- **Philosophical treatise**
- **Political manifesto**

---

## 🔹 B. Genre (Content Category)

### Fiction Genres

- Magical realism
- Romance
- Thriller

### Non-Fiction Genres

- Biography

- Science
- Journalism
- Memoir
