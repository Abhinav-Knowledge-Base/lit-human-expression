# William Shakespeare

## Overview
Shakespeare wrote 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and several narrative poems. His work is categorized into Comedies, Tragedies, Histories, and Romances.

## 🎭 1. Comedies
**Traits:** Humor, mistaken identities, romance, reconciliation, happy endings.
**Themes:** Love, gender roles, illusion vs reality, social harmony.
- **[A Midsummer Night's Dream](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Books/Western/4.%20Renaissance/A%20Midsummer%20Night's%20Dream.md):** Love as irrational and magical; illusion vs reality.
- **As You Like It:** Pastoral freedom; love & identity.
- **Twelfth Night:** Disguise, unrequited love, mistaken identity.
- **Much Ado About Nothing:** Wit, pride, misunderstanding in love.
- **The Merchant of Venice:** Justice, mercy, prejudice.
- **The Taming of the Shrew:** Gender dynamics & power.

## ⚔️ 2. Tragedies
**Traits:** Fatal flaw, downfall of protagonist, death.
**Themes:** Ambition, jealousy, betrayal, fate, moral corruption, existential doubt.
- **[Hamlet](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Books/Western/4.%20Renaissance/Hamlet.md):** Existential crisis, revenge, indecision.
- **Macbeth:** Ambition, guilt, power.
- **Othello:** Jealousy, manipulation, insecurity.
- **King Lear:** Authority, madness, filial betrayal.
- **Romeo and Juliet:** Passionate love vs social conflict.
- **Julius Caesar:** Politics, loyalty, ambition.

## 👑 3. Histories
**Traits:** Based on English kings and political events.
**Themes:** Power, legitimacy, leadership, national identity.
- **Richard III:** Manipulative ambition.
- **Henry V:** Leadership & patriotism.
- **Henry IV, Part 1**

## 🌊 4. Romances (Late Plays)
**Traits:** Blend of tragedy + comedy, redemption and forgiveness.
- **[The Tempest](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Books/Western/4.%20Renaissance/The%20Tempest.md):** Power, forgiveness, illusion.
- **The Winter's Tale:** Jealousy, loss, redemption.
- **Cymbeline**

## 📜 Sonnets
Explores beauty, time, mortality, desire, and immortality through art.
- **Sonnet 18:** "Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?"
