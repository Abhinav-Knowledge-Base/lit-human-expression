# The Locked Doors

There are doors inside us  
we never try to open.  
Not because we lost the keys—  
but because we know what’s inside.

The doors were always locked—  
not out of habit,  
but by agreement.

An agreement older than memory,  
passed quietly  
from one silence to another.

Some things, we decided,  
should never be seen.

In homes,  
there are rooms no one mentions—  
where truth sits like unused furniture,  
covered,  
respected,  
avoided.

At dinner tables,  
we learn which questions  
must remain questions.

In societies,  
there are stories  
that are told so often  
no one notices  
what is missing from them.

And in what we call faith,  
there are doors  
guarded not by locks,  
but by devotion—  
where doubt is not forbidden,  
just… unwelcome.

Even nations  
build corridors of pride  
with certain rooms sealed—  
history folded carefully,  
so it fits the narrative.

There are doors everywhere.  
Not hidden—  
just agreed upon.

Some we open without thinking.  
Some remain closed for years.

And some—  
we learn not to touch.

Not because they are locked,  
but because  
we locked them together.

And somewhere between  
silence and survival,  
we forgot  
who closed them first.
