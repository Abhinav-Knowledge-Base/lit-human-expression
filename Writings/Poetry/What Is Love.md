# What Is Love?

Is liking someone’s dress love?
Is admiring someone’s beauty love?
I see nothing extraordinary in my mother’s appearance — yet I love her.

Is talking for hours love?
Is vibe matching love?
I don’t talk endlessly with my mother.
Our “vibes” don’t match in the modern sense — yet I love her.

Is love escaping into a parallel universe of imagination?
Or is it just coffee dates and planned evenings?

Is it imagining old age together or sharing childhood memories?
Does choosing someone create love?
Or does love quietly grow without choice?

I did not choose my mother.
She did not choose me.
Yet love exists — unquestioned, unquestionable.

Is someone appearing in your dreams love?
Or caring for someone in real life?
Is it celebrating Valentine’s Day?
Or remembering her on Women’s Day?

Is it what Krishna felt in Nandgaon? 
Or what he lived in Dwarika?

Is it sharing daily chaos —
metro fights, office politics, traffic rush,
random gossip about strangers —
or silently observing life together?

Is it the fear of losing her?
Or gratitude for having her today?

Is it the cosmic coincidence —
that in a world of billions,
past, present, and future —
you met her?

Or is it the fight you had yesterday,
and the stubborn wait for a sorry?


When you wear a kurti and jeans with big earrings, and I say, “Aaj bahut khubsurat lag rahi ho,” is that love?
Or is it the confusion inside me — because you look beautiful always — and I still feel I must say it aloud?


Is it the excitement before meeting you —
or the ache that comes while saying goodbye?

Is it teasing you about your silly thoughts,
arguing just to see you react,
and secretly enjoying every second of it?

Is it understanding when your “haan” actually means “naa”?
Or when your “naa” quietly hides a “haan”?

Is it the intimacy of closeness?
Or the hurt when, in anger, you say,
“Don’t touch me”?

Is love being the center of my own world —
or slowly realizing
you have become the center of it?


So what is this feeling we seek?
Is it love?
Or just hormones dressed as poetry?

We define love loudly.
But I keep questioning it quietly.
Maybe it isn't what we says.
Maybe love is not a definition.
Maybe it is a presence.
