# Beyond Memory

She sits with me in every quiet room—
the one between two thoughts,
the one just before sleep.

Her laugh arrives
before I finish the sentence.
Her silence has a particular weight—
I know it the way you know weather
before it arrives.

We argue over nothing.

She holds her ground
like she always does—
arms folded,
eyes doing the rest.
I say sorry.

The room accepts it.

Her dislikes have grown into my habits.
I reach for things she'd reach for.
I leave things she'd leave.
Without trying,
I have become a little
of her shape.  

She is not a memory.
Memories fade at the edges.
She does not.

She is more like light
through a curtain—
not quite in the room,
but warm enough
that I don't reach for the lamp.
