# The Quiet Agreement

We don’t talk about it.

Not the doors we keep closed,
not the voices that argue inside.

We move through each other’s lives
as if everything is simple—
as if each of us is only one person.

But I have seen it now.

In pauses between sentences.
In the way someone almost speaks—
and then doesn’t.

In opinions held too firmly
and silences held even tighter.

Everyone is carrying something.
Not visible,
not named—
but present.

We call it personality.
We call it belief.
We call it nature.

Anything
but what it really is.

A quiet negotiation
between all that lives inside us.

I have stopped expecting consistency.

The same person
who speaks with certainty today
will doubt tomorrow.

The one who resists
will surrender.

The one who hides
will, sometimes,
want to be seen.

And none of it is false.

It is just… incomplete.

We are not singular enough
to be understood once and for all.

So we adjust.

We listen partially.
We forgive selectively.
We understand just enough
to keep things moving.

Not perfectly—
but sufficiently.

Maybe that is what living together is.

Not complete understanding.
Not perfect honesty.

Just a silent agreement—

that we will not force open
each other’s locked doors,

that we will not question
every contradiction,

that we will allow
each other
to remain
slightly unknown.

And still—

stay.
