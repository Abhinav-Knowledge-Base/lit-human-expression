# Is This Love, or Just Care?

Is it love,  
or just care?

They say love comes naturally.  
Then why does hate  
need reasons,  
conditions,  
justifications?

I don’t struggle with most people.  
I don’t carry anger for them.  
There is a quiet ease—  
a simple, human softness.

Is that love?

And if it is,  
then what makes you different?

Is it that I care for you more,  
or that I call it love  
just because it feels deeper,  
louder,  
harder to ignore?

This question  
doesn’t let me sleep.

Because sometimes,  
it feels like the answer is simple—

That love is special,  
rare,  
set apart.

And then,  
in a moment of anger,  
a careless argument,  
a raised voice—

everything collapses.

After all the time,  
all the memories,  
all the silent understandings—

how can something called love  
still carry doubt?

So I wonder—

Is love truly natural?  
Or is it something  
we slowly build,  
layer by layer,  
moment by moment?

And if it is built,  
then what is this quiet care  
that exists for everyone else?

Is that the real nature—  
and love  
just a name we give  
when it becomes  
too intense to ignore?

I keep circling back  
to the same place—

Trying to understand  
which feeling is honest,  
and which one  
we learn to create.

Which one is natural—  
and which one  
we manufacture  
to make meaning  
out of closeness.
