# Answers No One Asked For

Tonight, I walked those streets again—
the same ones…
where we once existed together.

No one asked me anything.
But everything did.

The bench felt familiar,
like it remembered the weight of us…
and quietly wondered
why it holds only one now.

The neem breeze touched my skin
the same way it used to—
but lingered longer,
as if searching for someone it once knew.

The silence of the road
wasn’t empty—
it was full of unfinished conversations.

That ice cream stall,
still open at the edge of the night,
felt like a habit that forgot to end.

Even the tea stall
seemed dimmer somehow—
like a moon
without its light.

The dog you loved
stood there the same…
only I wasn’t.

And the birds, the stars,
the faint fragrance of night—
nothing had changed.

Still, nothing was the same.

I stayed there for a while…
as if something might return.

But the night moved on.

And I…
walked back
with answers
no one had asked for.
