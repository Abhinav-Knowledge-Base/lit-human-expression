# Closed Files, Open Questions

There are files in the world  
that shake us when they open.

The kind that make headlines,  
the kind that make us say—  
“This is wrong. This must be punished.”

And for a moment,  
we feel certain  
about justice.

But then a quieter thought arrives—

What about the files  
I carry within me?

Not written anywhere,  
not recorded,  
not exposed—  
just… known.

Known only to me.

And maybe,  
you have them too.

So who decides  
what deserves punishment  
and what escapes silence?

Who draws that line  
between wrong and right—  
the one who is caught,  
or the one who is not?

We say humans stand  
at the center of this world.

But who gave us that position?

The same humans  
who write laws,  
argue in courts,  
defend the guilty,  
protect the innocent—  
and sometimes,  
confuse the two.

We fight for animals,  
take their suffering  
to the highest courts.

We build arguments,  
we pass judgments,  
we call it justice.

But all of it—  
written, argued, decided—  
by us.

Then comes morality.

The vegetarian  
who draws the line at chicken.  
The monk  
who tries to save every living being,  
yet knows  
it cannot be fully lived.

So what is this morality  
we speak of every day?

Is it universal?  
Or is it… convenient?

We call this  
the age of science.

And science tells us—  
there was no grand plan.

No script.  
No intention.

Just chance.

A random unfolding  
where somehow  
we became conscious,  
powerful,  
dominant.

Not chosen—  
just… happened.

And yet,  
we behave  
as if we were meant  
to decide everything.

Who gets to live.  
What is right.  
What is wrong.

Do plants not have a will to live?  
Or do they stay outside morality  
just because  
they are silent?

Maybe morality  
is not as absolute  
as we want it to be.

Maybe it is shaped  
by the majority,  
by convenience,  
by survival.

Or maybe—  
it is just  
the story we tell ourselves  
to sleep at night  
with our own files  
left unopened.

And somewhere  
between judgment and silence,  
I find myself stuck—

In an endless loop  
of questions.

What is right?  
What is wrong?  
What is moral?  
What is not?

And more importantly—

Who am I  
to decide?
