# I Live With Thousands of Strangers

Whenever my parents ask,
“Do you have friends there?”
I smile and say, “Yes.”

But the truth is different.

I live among thousands of strangers —
faces in the metro,
voices at the chai tapri,
silhouettes stuck beside me in traffic,
people passing by in autos.

They are not my friends in the usual sense.
We don’t sit for hours sharing long stories —
stories full of fiction and reality,
serious talks and silly jokes,
truths mixed with exaggerations.

And yet, I feel close to them in a strange way.

Some are at the last stage of their love.
Some are just beginning theirs.
Some carry marital silence.
Some hide emotional storms.
Some are tired of office politics.
Some are simply lost in thought.

We never exchange names.
We never promise to meet again.
But in small, fleeting moments,
their reality brushes against mine.

Yes, I live with thousands of strangers —
friends I never formally met,
yet companions in this shared journey of life,
each carrying a short but real story.
