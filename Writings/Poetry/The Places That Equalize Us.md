# The Places That Equalize Us

There are only certain places
that equalize us—
A hospital,
a place of prayer,
and a constitution.

What is common
in these places?

In a hospital,
pain has no status.
The body does not ask
who you are
before it breaks.

A rich man bleeds the same,
a poor man fears the same.
For a moment,
we are just human.

In a place of prayer,
we bow our heads
to something greater.
Names, titles, identities—
left quietly
outside the door.
For a moment,
we are just seekers.

And in a constitution,
we write down equality—
carefully,
deliberately,
as a promise.
A promise
that every life
holds the same value.
For a moment,
we believe it.

But outside these places,
the world returns
to its differences.

Power, privilege, identity—
they all find their way back.

So I wonder—
Do these places
truly equalize us?
Or do they just remind us
of what we could be,
but choose not to be?

Maybe equality
is not a reality—
but a brief moment
we experience
when everything else
is stripped away.
