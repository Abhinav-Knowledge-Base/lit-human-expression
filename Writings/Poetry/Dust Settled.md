# Dust Settled

Dust settled I watched myself wait.
Hands that forgot what to do with themselves,
eyes that kept finding the clock
as if time needed to be checked,
as if time cared.

There is a version of us
that lives just before the answer —
and that version believes, quietly, desperately,
that the universe is listening.
That something out there
is weighing our wanting
against its own vast indifference.

We make deals in that silence.
Not with God necessarily.
With something.
With the air.

Just this once.
Just let this one thing go right.

And then the moment comes.
Not dramatically.
It never does.

A score on a screen.
A name on a list.
A ball hitting the ground
or clearing it —

and the world splits
into the one where it went well
and the one where it didn't.

Both feel like they were always inevitable.
Neither feels survivable.
At first.

Then comes the world.
People with their faces
and their words
and their versions of what happened
and what it means
and what you should do now.

You nod.
You say the things.
You perform the recovering
before the recovering has actually begun.

Inside —
something is still sitting
in that moment before the answer.
Still waiting.
Still making deals.

And then, slowly.
Not healing exactly.
More like — the volume lowers.

The thing that felt like the entire sky
becomes a chapter.
Then a paragraph.
Then a sentence you can say out loud
without your voice changing.

You look back
and see yourself there —
that person who believed
the universe hinged on this —
and you feel something
that isn't quite pity
and isn't quite love.
Something quieter than both.

The dust settled.
Not because the storm was resolved.
But because storms
don't last forever,
even the ones we are convinced will.

And what remains —
after the waiting, the moment, the world, the noise —
is just you.

Slightly different.
Carrying it differently.
Still here.

Which, on most days,
is enough.
