# How Many People Live Inside Me

How many people live inside me?

One carries questions—
endless, unfinished.

Another has answers
for everything.

One challenges what is accepted—
pulls it apart,
questions its certainty.

Another knows what is right—
and still chooses otherwise.

The ambitious one dreams loudly.
The realistic one
watches him
with a faint smile.

One wants to speak—
on everything,
to be heard.

Another sits back—
observing,
almost detached.

And both feel like me.

One wants ambition.
One wants peace.
One wants love.
One wants isolation.

One part of me wants everything.
Another
is tired of wanting at all.

I was not born as one person.
I was assembled—
slowly,
by voices that were never mine.

I speak, I laugh, I react—
and somewhere inside,
someone watches all of it
without participating.

I am calm outside—
and a storm
that never quite breaks inside.

I am not one person—
I am a conversation
that never ends.

I thought the conflict was mine.

Until I started seeing it outside—
in arguments that never end,
in beliefs that refuse to bend,
in people
just as certain
and just as divided.

I thought the noise was mine.

Until I heard the same noise
everywhere.

I don’t know
whether I carry the world inside me—
or I am just
one small reflection of it.

Maybe I am not divided.

Maybe I am just Human.
