# The Ghost I Left Behind

The city hasn’t changed its pulse,
the same stray shadows
still pace the corners of the park.

I see you there—
a silhouette against the yellow streetlamps,
retracing steps we took
when the world felt small enough for us.

You think the bench is empty,
but I left a part of me there
to wait for you.

It doesn’t speak;
it only watches
the way you pull your coat tighter
against a wind that still remembers your name.

I saw the tea stall too—
the steam still rises in slow spirals,
carrying the words we never finished.

They aren’t lost;
they’re just buried in the noise of the traffic,
waiting for a silence deep enough
to hear them again.

You walked back with answers.
I stayed back with the questions.

And the moon, still dim,
just hangs there—
as if it knows
some things don’t return.
