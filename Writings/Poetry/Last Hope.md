# Last Hope

There is a particular kind of quiet
that lives just before everything.

Not peace.
Don't mistake it for peace.

It is the quiet of a held breath,
of a hand gripping something
it may have to let go of soon.

We call it hope
but it doesn't feel like hope.

It feels like standing at the edge
of something you cannot see the bottom of
and deciding —
just this once —
to believe the ground is there.

Not because you know.
Because you have to.

This is where we are most human, I think.
Not in our victories.
Not even in our grief.

But here —
in this suspended, trembling place
where we are still allowed to want,
where the answer hasn't arrived yet
to take that away from us.

The last hope isn't loud.
It doesn't announce itself.

It sits in the chest
like something small and warm
that knows
it may not make it to morning.

And still —
it stays.
