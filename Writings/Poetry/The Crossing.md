# The Crossing

We met at the threshold of the park,
where the yellow light spills
and the shadows finally stop pacing.

I was the one who stayed,
and you were the one who came back
to see if the silence still held our shape.

For a moment, the wind died down.
The steam from the stall froze in the air,
and the questions I kept
finally met the answers you carried—
heavy, unasked-for,
and cold as the bench between us.

You didn’t see me,
but you felt the space get smaller.
You didn't hear me,
but you adjusted your coat
as if my ghost had finally
let go of your sleeve.

There is no more "us" to guard now.
The dog has turned the corner,
the ice cream melt has dried on the pavement,
and the moon has seen enough.

I am not a haunting anymore;
I am just a memory
stepping out of the light.

You walk toward the morning,
and I finally
stop waiting.
