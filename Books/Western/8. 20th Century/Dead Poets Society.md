# Dead Poets Society (1989)

- **Directed by:** Peter Weir
- **Starring:** Robin Williams as John Keating

## 1. Setting & Atmosphere
Welton Academy (1959), a rigid boys' boarding school built on **Tradition, Honor, Discipline, and Excellence**. It represents institutional authority and emotional restraint.

## 2. John Keating & "Carpe Diem"
A non-conformist teacher who inspires students to think independently. 
- **Motto:** "Carpe Diem. Seize the day, boys. Make your lives extraordinary."
- **Belief:** Poetry is about passion and rebellion, not just metrics.

## 3. The Students
- **Neil Perry:** Talented but suffocated by an authoritarian father. Plays Puck in **[A Midsummer Night's Dream](file:///d:/Miscellaneous/Books/Western/4.%20Renaissance/A%20Midsummer%20Night's%20Dream.md)**.
- **Todd Anderson:** Shy and insecure, finding his voice through Keating.
- **The Cave:** The secret meeting place for the Dead Poets Society, symbolizing a space of freedom.

## 4. Tragedy & Aftermath
The clash between Neil's passion (acting) and his father's expectations leads to Neil's suicide. Keating is scapegoated and fired, but his influence remains as seen in the final "O Captain! My Captain!" salute.

## 5. Core Themes
- **Individuality vs Conformity:** The unique human being vs the identical success story.
- **Authority vs Freedom:** Institutional control vs personal passion.
- **The Power of Art:** Poetry as emotional truth and rebellion.
- **Existentialism:** The mandate to create meaning in one's life.

## 6. Philosophical Connection
Connects to **Individualism** and **Existentialism**. Neil's tragedy shows what happens when fear crushes identity.
